# identity-example — Lucía, the cat F designer with a project about to tip her over

> Composite — not a real client. Built from the pattern of CABA freelancers who bill a steady US client in USD and hit the monotributo ceiling not by growth but by one fat invoice landing in the wrong month. The STRUCTURE-mode user.

## Who

Lucía, product designer in CABA. Monotributo (Argentine simplified tax regime) **cat F**. One anchor US SaaS client on a monthly retainer plus smaller US gigs — 100% export, all `Factura E` (export-services invoice), `IIBB` (provincial gross-income tax) CABA (export-exempt). Rolling-12 ~USD 30,000, steady at ~USD 3,500/month. Has a `contador` (accountant) she emails a couple of times a year; nothing regular. Owns no property; keeps dollars in a US account and a local bank box. She is *organized* — she knows her category, knows she's close — and that's exactly why she's the clean case.

## The decision they're on the hook for

A single project closes end of July, USD 8,000–9,000 **in one shot**, on top of her standing run-rate. The question is structural and annual, not monthly: **does this one invoice push her rolling-12 over the cat F ceiling, and if so, what does she do before ARCA does it for her?** This is not "which channel do I cash the project in" — that's her routing coach's question. It's "what category am I in after July, and when do I move."

## Voice pillars

1. **Plain-anxious, never panicked.** Sounds like: *"Am I about to blow past my category? Do I need to do anything before I get in trouble?"* Not: *"what's my marginal effective rate"* — she doesn't talk in tax abstractions, she talks in "trouble" and "before."
2. **Self-quantified in ranges.** She brings her own numbers and hedges them honestly: *"about USD 30,000, pretty steady at ~3,500/month,"* *"somewhere between USD 8K and 9K."* A person who tracks her invoices, not one who guesses.
3. **Pre-emptive.** She asks *before* the deadline, not after the notice: *"Do I need to do anything before I get in trouble?"* The whole point of her is she wants to act early — she just needs the exact move and the exact window.

## Vocabulary

- Says: *"blow past my category," "big project closing," "in one shot," "my normal monthly run-rate," "all Factura E," "IIBB CABA (exempt, I think)," "nothing fancy," "before I get in trouble," "the contador I email a couple times a year."*
- Close cousins to avoid (she does **not** talk like this): *"fiscal optimization," "regime arbitrage," "marginal bracket," "tax-efficient structure."* If the output reaches for advisor-speak, it isn't answering Lucía.
- AR-tax proper nouns stay Spanish even in her English thread: `monotributo`, `cat F/G`, `recategorización`, `Factura E`, `IIBB`, `recat de oficio`, `Bienes Personales`, `contador`.

## Content themes

1. **The one-shot invoice that tips the rolling-12.** Her ceiling risk is episodic, not a slow climb — one USD 8–9K project lands and the 12-month total crosses. The structural read: the crossing is computed once against the ceiling, not felt invoice-by-invoice.
2. **Recategorize proactively vs wait for ARCA.** Her instinct ("before I get in trouble") *is* the fork: a proactive `recategorización` (the semi-annual category move) in the winter window vs eating a `recat de oficio` (ARCA forcibly recategorizing you) with its up-to-50% penalty. She wants permission to move early; the right answer gives it.
3. **Where the dollars sit at 31/12.** She holds USD in a US account and a local box. The annual lever — USD in an Argentine account at year-end is exento for `Bienes Personales` (wealth tax) — is free money she's nowhere near needing yet, but it's the kind of forward note she values.

## Decision posture

Lucía arrives roughly right and wants confirmation plus the exact move — not a menu. The specialist gives her a deterministic, committed call:

- Rolling-12 **$34.500.000,00** vs cat F ceiling **$38.642.048,36** = **89,3%** → tier 🔴 **ROJO**. Headroom **$4.142.048,36**.
- The July project (**$9.200.000,00–$10.350.000,00**) overruns the entire headroom by itself → the rolling-12 crosses into cat G in **agosto 2026**. One-step F→G, not an RI question.
- **Verdict: stay in monotributo, recategorize proactively to cat G in the winter window (closes 5-ago-2026).** Cuota goes **$129.045,32/mes → $197.108,23/mes** — that's the price, and it's the cheap outcome.
- **Bienes Personales:** patrimony far below MNI **$384.728.044,57** → no obligation.
- **Confidence: HIGH** — all 6 inputs clean, the call is pure arithmetic from a current reference figure, and it's a deterministic *trigger*, not a complex choice.
- **Escalation:** none active today. It fires only if she takes local AR clients (mixed income) or her rolling-12 approaches the cat-K límite **$108.357.084,05** — neither in play at USD 30K/year.

The only way Lucía gets hurt is by waiting. The posture is: commit to F→G, hand her the window, get out of the way.

## What the specialist does differently for her

Because the inputs are clean and the trigger is deterministic, it commits **HIGH** and refuses to muddy the call with RI talk she doesn't need — she's nowhere near cat K, so the RI cost model never appears. It gives her a calendar month (agosto 2026) and the exact cuota delta, not "you might want to consider." It explicitly **does not** tell her which channel to bring her US-account dollars home through — that's the routing sibling, and it says so. And it writes the Reviewer Brief so her once-a-year `contador` email becomes a 10-minute confirmation instead of a discovery call.

## Why this profile exists

*Lucía is the clean deterministic-trigger case — the proof that the specialist commits to a HIGH structural call (the category, the month, the cuota) when the arithmetic is unambiguous, instead of hedging into a menu. She is the flagship thesis demo, and her figures are pinned identically to JUDGE_GUIDE Test 1.*
