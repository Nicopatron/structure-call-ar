# Brief — The structure call I keep getting wrong

**The problem (real, mine, right now).** I'm an Argentine indie consultant invoicing US clients in USD. This month a client payment pushes my rolling-12 invoicing toward the ceiling of my current monotributo category. Every Argentine freelancer billing in dollars hits the same wall — and it's not the one people talk about. The "which payment channel" question is the easy one. The one that costs you is structural and episodic: recategorize proactively or wait for ARCA's official pass? Am I about to be forced out of monotributo into Responsable Inscripto (RI) — IVA, Ganancias, a monthly contador bill? Is my Factura E setup correct for service exports? Does IIBB apply in my province? And the one I most often get wrong: at what exact point does this stop being my call and become my contador's?

**What I tried.** My contador every time — slow, reactive, answers once it's already late. Generic ChatGPT — invents AFIP regs, no calibration date, no refusal. A spreadsheet — math, no judgment, no escalation logic.

**What I need.** A folder-specialist that, given my category, USD volume, rolling-12 projection, client type, IIBB jurisdiction, and contador status, computes the *trigger* (the month I cross a threshold) and the *structural verdict* — recategorize, or prepare the RI jump — then escalates to a contador the moment the choice turns binding. Not a channel router. A structure call.
